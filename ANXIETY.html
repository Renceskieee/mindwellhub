Anxiety

What is Anxiety?

Anxiety is an unsettling emotional state characterized by inner turmoil and the anticipation of future threats, distinct from fear, which responds to real threats. It involves generalized and unfocused worry often accompanied by nervous behaviors and physical symptoms like muscular tension, restlessness, and fatigue. Linked to fear but centered on expecting future threats, anxiety can persist and evolve into anxiety disorders, such as generalized anxiety disorder or panic disorder, where the emotional distress extends beyond typical timeframes.

Symptoms

While most individuals do not experience chronic anxiety, it can lead to psychological distress, mental disorders, and even self-harm or suicidal thoughts.

The physiological symptoms of anxiety may include:

Neurological, such as headache, paresthesias, fasciculations, vertigo, or presyncope.

Digestive, such as abdominal pain, nausea, diarrhea, indigestion, dry mouth, or bolus.

Stress hormones released in an anxious state have an impact on bowel function and can manifest physical symptoms that may contribute to or exacerbate IBS.

Respiratory, such as shortness of breath or sighing breathing. Cardiac, as palpitations, tachycardia, or chest pain.
